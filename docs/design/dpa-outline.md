# Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Outline

**Status:** Outline Complete
**Last Updated:** December 24, 2025
**Version:** 1.0
**Purpose:** Comprehensive outline for the platform's Data Processing Agreement

## Document Purpose

This Data Processing Agreement (DPA) establishes the terms under which the platform processes personal data on behalf of customers. Required by GDPR Article 28 and expected by enterprise customers for compliance with international data protection laws.

**When Required:**
- **Mandatory**: For all EU/EEA/UK customers under GDPR
- **Standard Practice**: For Team and Enterprise plans (B2B customers)
- **Upon Request**: For Pro plan customers who need it for compliance

---

### 1. Definitions

**Purpose**: Define key terms for clarity and legal precision

**Key Definitions**:

#### 1.1 Data Protection Law
> "'Data Protection Law' means all applicable laws and regulations relating to privacy and data protection, including:
> - The EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (Regulation (EU) 2016/679)
> - The UK GDPR and UK Data Protection Act 2018
> - The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)
> - The Brazilian General Data Protection Law (LGPD)
> - Any other applicable privacy or data protection law"

#### 1.2 Personal Data
> "'Personal Data' means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that is uploaded to or processed through the Service by Customer, including but not limited to:
> - Names, email addresses, and contact information contained in documents or comments
> - Account information of Customer's users (Authorized Users)
> - Any other information that qualifies as 'personal data' under Data Protection Law"

**Examples**:
- HTML documents containing employee names and contact information
- User profiles (name, email, job title)
- Comments and annotations by identifiable users
- IP addresses and usage logs (when linked to identifiable users)

**Exclusions**:
- Aggregated, anonymized data that cannot be linked to individuals
- Customer company name and workspace name (not personal data unless sole proprietorship)

#### 1.3 Processing
> "'Processing' means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, storage, retrieval, use, disclosure, deletion, or destruction."

#### 1.4 Controller
> "'Controller' means the entity that determines the purposes and means of Processing Personal Data. In this DPA, Customer is the Controller."

#### 1.5 Processor
> "'Processor' means the entity that Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. In this DPA, Company is the Processor."

#### 1.6 Sub-processor
> "'Sub-processor' means any third party engaged by Company to Process Personal Data on behalf of Customer. Examples: cloud infrastructure providers (AWS, Convex), analytics services, email delivery services."

#### 1.7 Data Subject
> "'Data Subject' means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is Processed. Examples: Customer's employees, contractors, or other individuals whose information appears in documents uploaded to the Service."

#### 1.8 Supervisory Authority
> "'Supervisory Authority' means an independent public authority established by an EU Member State to oversee GDPR compliance. Examples: CNIL (France), ICO (UK), Irish DPC (Ireland)."

#### 1.9 Authorized Users
> "'Authorized Users' means individuals authorized by Customer to use the Service, including workspace administrators, document creators, and reviewers."

### 2. Scope and Roles

**Purpose**: Clarify data processing relationship

#### 2.1 Processor Relationship

**Company Acts as Processor**:
> "Company will Process Personal Data only as a Processor on behalf of and in accordance with Customer's documented instructions. Customer is the Controller and is responsible for compliance with Data Protection Law in its role as Controller."

**Customer as Controller**:
- Customer determines what Personal Data is uploaded to the Service
- Customer determines who has access (Authorized Users, reviewers)
- Customer controls retention and deletion of documents and comments
- Customer is responsible for obtaining necessary consents from Data Subjects

**Company as Processor**:
- Company processes Personal Data solely to provide the Service
- Company follows Customer's instructions (as documented in this DPA and the Service interface)
- Company does not use Personal Data for its own purposes (except as permitted by law, e.g., aggregated analytics)

#### 2.2 Customer Instructions

**Documented Instructions**:
> "Customer instructs Company to Process Personal Data as necessary to:
> 1. Provide the Service as described in the Terms of Service
> 2. Comply with Customer's configuration settings (e.g., sharing permissions, retention settings)
> 3. Respond to Customer's support requests
> 4. Comply with applicable laws"

**Additional Instructions**:
- Customer may provide additional written instructions via email to: dpo@[platform].com
- Company will confirm feasibility within 5 business days
- Additional instructions may incur additional fees if they require significant changes to the Service

**Unlawful Instructions**:
- If Company believes an instruction violates Data Protection Law, Company will promptly notify Customer
- Company may refuse to carry out the instruction until resolved

### 3. Details of Processing

**Purpose**: Describe processing activities (GDPR Article 28(3) requirement)

#### 3.1 Subject Matter of Processing

**What is Processed**:
- Personal Data contained in HTML documents uploaded by Customer
- Comments, annotations, and feedback created by Authorized Users
- Account information of Authorized Users (name, email, profile data)
- Usage data and activity logs related to the Service

#### 3.2 Duration of Processing

**Processing Period**:
> "Company will Process Personal Data for the duration of the Service subscription and for 30 days thereafter (backup retention period), unless Customer requests earlier deletion or Data Protection Law requires longer retention."

**Post-Termination**:
- Upon subscription termination, Company will delete or return Personal Data within 30 days (see Section 9)
- Backup copies deleted within 90 days

#### 3.3 Nature and Purpose of Processing

**Nature**:
- Hosting and storage of HTML documents and associated data
- Displaying documents to Authorized Users
- Facilitating collaboration (comments, sharing, notifications)
- Generating version history
- Providing analytics and activity logs
- Delivering email and in-app notifications

**Purpose**:
- To provide the Service as described in the Terms of Service
- To enable team collaboration on HTML documents
- To provide support and troubleshoot issues
- To maintain security and prevent abuse

#### 3.4 Type of Personal Data

**Categories of Personal Data**:
- **Identifiers**: Names, email addresses, usernames, IP addresses
- **Professional information**: Job titles, company names (when provided)
- **User-generated content**: HTML documents, comments, annotations, uploaded files
- **Usage data**: Pages viewed, features used, access times, device information
- **Communication data**: Email addresses for notifications, Slack usernames (if integrated)

**Sensitive Data**:
- Company does not intentionally collect sensitive personal data (also called "special categories" under GDPR: racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, sex life or sexual orientation)
- **Customer Responsibility**: If Customer uploads documents containing sensitive data, Customer is responsible for ensuring lawful basis and additional safeguards

#### 3.5 Categories of Data Subjects

**Data Subjects**:
- Authorized Users: Employees, contractors, or agents of Customer who use the Service
- Document creators: Individuals who upload HTML documents
- Reviewers: Individuals invited to view and comment on documents (internal or external to Customer's organization)
- Other individuals: Any person whose Personal Data appears in uploaded documents (e.g., stakeholders mentioned in documents, customers, vendors)

### 4. Security Measures

**Purpose**: Detail technical and organizational safeguards (GDPR Article 32)

#### 4.1 Technical Measures

**Encryption**:
- **In transit**: TLS 1.3 encryption for all data transmitted over networks
- **At rest**: AES-256 encryption for all data stored in databases and file storage

**Access Controls**:
- **Role-based access control (RBAC)**: Users can only access data they are authorized to view
- **Authentication**: Multi-factor authentication (2FA) available; required for Enterprise administrators
- **Least privilege**: Company employees have access only to data necessary for their role

**Network Security**:
- **Firewalls**: Network segmentation and perimeter firewalls
- **Intrusion detection**: Monitoring for unauthorized access attempts
- **Regular security updates**: Patching and updates applied promptly

**Data Segregation**:
- **Logical isolation**: Each Customer's data is logically segregated in the database
- **No cross-customer access**: Technical controls prevent one Customer from accessing another's data

#### 4.2 Organizational Measures

**Employee Training**:
- **Security and privacy training**: All employees with data access complete annual training
- **Confidentiality obligations**: All employees sign confidentiality agreements

**Background Checks**:
- **Pre-employment screening**: Background checks for employees with access to Customer data

**Access Management**:
- **Principle of least privilege**: Access granted only as necessary for job function
- **Access reviews**: Quarterly reviews to ensure appropriate access levels
- **Offboarding**: Immediate revocation of access upon employee termination

**Incident Response**:
- **Incident response plan**: Documented procedures for security incidents
- **Breach notification**: Procedures to notify Customer within 72 hours (see Section 8)
- **Regular drills**: Annual incident response tabletop exercises

#### 4.3 Physical Security

**Data Centers**:
- **SOC 2 compliant facilities**: Data hosted in facilities with SOC 2 Type II certification (via AWS/Convex)
- **Physical access controls**: Biometric access, surveillance, security guards
- **Environmental controls**: Fire suppression, cooling, uninterruptible power supply (UPS)

**Company Offices**:
- **Secure facilities**: Locked offices, badge access, visitor logs
- **Device security**: Encrypted laptops, remote wipe capability, screen locks

#### 4.4 Data Backup and Disaster Recovery

**Backups**:
- **Frequency**: Automated daily backups
- **Encryption**: Backups encrypted with same standards as production data
- **Retention**: Backups retained for 30 days (configurable for Enterprise)

**Disaster Recovery**:
- **Business continuity plan**: Documented procedures for disaster recovery
- **RTO/RPO**: Target Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of 4 hours, Recovery Point Objective (RPO) of 1 hour (Enterprise)
- **Testing**: Annual disaster recovery drills

#### 4.5 Certifications and Audits

**Current Status**:
- Hosted on SOC 2 compliant infrastructure (Convex/AWS)
- Compliance with industry standards (OWASP, NIST)

**Planned Certifications**:
- **SOC 2 Type I**: [Target date: Q2 2025]
- **SOC 2 Type II**: [Target date: Q4 2025]
- **ISO 27001**: [Under consideration]

**Audit Rights** (see Section 10):

### 6. Data Subject Rights

**Purpose**: Assist Customer in fulfilling Data Subject rights requests

#### 6.1 Customer Responsibility

**Controller Obligations**:
> "As the Controller, Customer is responsible for responding to Data Subject requests to exercise their rights under Data Protection Law (e.g., access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection)."

**Customer's Tools**:
- Customer can fulfill most requests via the Service interface:
  - **Access**: Export documents and user data
  - **Rectification**: Edit documents, update user profiles
  - **Erasure**: Delete documents, delete user accounts
  - **Portability**: Export data in portable format (HTML, JSON)

#### 6.2 Company Assistance

**Company Will Assist**:
> "To the extent Customer cannot fulfill a Data Subject request using the Service interface, Company will provide reasonable assistance, taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to Company."

**How Company Assists**:
- **Technical assistance**: Help Customer locate or export specific data
- **Respond to inquiries**: Answer questions about data processing
- **Custom exports**: Provide data exports in specific formats (if technically feasible)

**Request Process**:
1. Customer contacts Company at: dsr@[platform].com or support@[platform].com
2. Customer provides details: Data Subject identity, type of request, data in question
3. Company responds within **5 business days** with assistance or exports
4. Customer is responsible for verifying Data Subject identity and responding to Data Subject

**Company Fees**:
- Assistance for reasonable requests: No fee
- Excessive or repetitive requests: Company may charge reasonable administrative fees

#### 6.3 Direct Requests to Company

**If Data Subject Contacts Company Directly**:
- Company will promptly notify Customer (within **2 business days**)
- Company will not respond directly to Data Subject without Customer's prior authorization (unless required by law)
- Customer is responsible for responding to Data Subject

### 7. Confidentiality

**Purpose**: Ensure Personal Data is kept confidential

#### 7.1 Employee Confidentiality

**Obligation**:
> "Company will ensure that all personnel with access to Personal Data are subject to confidentiality obligations (contractual or statutory) and receive appropriate training on data protection."

**Who Has Access**:
- Engineers: For service maintenance and troubleshooting
- Support staff: To respond to Customer support requests
- Security team: To monitor and respond to security incidents
- Executives: Limited access for business purposes

**Limitations**:
- Access restricted to personnel who need it for their job function
- No access for personal use or curiosity
- Violation of confidentiality is grounds for disciplinary action, including termination

#### 7.2 No Disclosure

**Company Will Not Disclose**:
- Company will not disclose Personal Data to third parties except:
  - To Sub-processors (per Section 5)
  - To Customer or as instructed by Customer
  - As required by law (see Section 7.3)

#### 7.3 Legal Compulsion

**If Company Receives Legal Demand**:
- Company will notify Customer promptly (within **24 hours** if possible) before disclosing Personal Data, unless:
  - Legally prohibited from notifying (e.g., gag order)
  - Disclosure is to law enforcement in connection with imminent threat to safety

**Company Will**:
- Challenge overly broad or improper requests (where reasonable)
- Disclose only the minimum Personal Data required to comply
- Provide Customer with copy of legal demand (if not prohibited)

### 8. Data Breach Notification

**Purpose**: Establish breach notification procedures (GDPR Article 33-34)

#### 8.1 Definition of Personal Data Breach

**Breach Means**:
> "A 'Personal Data Breach' means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data."

**Examples**:
- Unauthorized access to Customer's documents or account
- Data exfiltration by attacker
- Accidental public exposure of documents
- Loss of backup media
- Ransomware attack

#### 8.2 Notification to Customer

**Timing**:
> "Company will notify Customer of a Personal Data Breach without undue delay and, where feasible, **within 72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach."

**Notification Method**:
- Email to Customer's account administrator
- If urgent, also by phone or in-Service notification

**Notification Contents**:
- Description of the breach (what happened, when, how discovered)
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected
- Categories and approximate number of records affected
- Likely consequences of the breach
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach
- Measures taken or proposed to mitigate potential adverse effects
- Contact point for further information (dpo@[platform].com)

**Updates**:
- Company will provide updates as investigation progresses

#### 8.3 Customer's Obligations

**Customer's Responsibility**:
- Customer is responsible for notifying affected Data Subjects (if required by Data Protection Law)
- Customer is responsible for notifying Supervisory Authorities (if required by Data Protection Law)

**GDPR Timeline**:
- Under GDPR, Controllers must notify Supervisory Authorities within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach
- Company's notification to Customer enables Customer to meet this timeline

#### 8.4 Breach Investigation

**Company Will**:
- Investigate the breach to determine cause and scope
- Implement measures to prevent recurrence
- Cooperate with Customer's investigation (if any)
- Provide reasonable assistance to Customer in notifying Supervisory Authorities and Data Subjects

### 9. Data Deletion and Return

**Purpose**: Define data handling upon termination

#### 9.1 Deletion Upon Termination

**When Subscription Ends**:
> "Upon termination or expiration of the Service subscription, Company will delete or return all Personal Data (at Customer's choice) within **30 days**, unless Data Protection Law requires continued storage."

**Customer's Choice**:
- **Delete**: Company deletes all Personal Data (recommended for most Customers)
- **Return**: Company exports and provides Personal Data to Customer, then deletes

**How to Request Return**:
- Contact: support@[platform].com before or within 30 days of termination
- Specify format if you have a preference (HTML, JSON, CSV)

#### 9.2 Backup Deletion

**Backup Copies**:
- Backup systems may retain Personal Data for up to **90 days** after termination
- Backups are overwritten on rolling basis
- Company cannot selectively delete from backups

**Secure Deletion**:
- Personal Data is securely deleted (overwritten or cryptographic erasure)
- Deletion makes data unrecoverable

#### 9.3 Legal Retention

**Exceptions**:
- If Data Protection Law requires retention (e.g., tax records, litigation hold), Company may retain Personal Data to extent and for duration required
- Company will notify Customer of retained data and duration
- Retained data remains subject to confidentiality obligations

#### 9.4 Certification of Deletion

**Upon Request**:
- Customer may request written certification that Personal Data has been deleted
- Company will provide certification within **10 business days**

### 10. Audit Rights

**Purpose**: Enable Customer to verify compliance (GDPR Article 28(3)(h))

#### 10.1 Customer's Audit Rights

**Right to Audit**:
> "Customer has the right to audit Company's compliance with this DPA, subject to the conditions in this Section 10."

**Why**:
- GDPR Article 28 requires Processor to allow Controller to conduct audits
- Enterprise customers often require audit rights for their own compliance

#### 10.2 Audit by Documentation Review

**First Step**:
- Customer may request documentation of Company's data protection practices
- Company will provide:
  - Copy of current SOC 2 report (when available)
  - Copy of security policies and procedures (summary)
  - List of Sub-processors and their DPAs
  - Evidence of security measures (without revealing vulnerabilities)

**Timeline**:
- Company will provide documentation within **30 days** of request

**Frequency**:
- Once per year (unless there is a suspected breach or compliance issue)

#### 10.3 On-Site Audit

**If Documentation Insufficient**:
- Customer may conduct an on-site audit or engage an independent third-party auditor

**Conditions**:
- **Notice**: At least **60 days'** advance written notice
- **Frequency**: No more than once per year (unless suspected breach or regulatory requirement)
- **Scope**: Audit limited to Company's compliance with this DPA
- **Confidentiality**: Auditor must sign non-disclosure agreement (NDA)
- **Timing**: During business hours, with minimal disruption to Company operations
- **Costs**: Customer bears all costs of audit

**Company Cooperation**:
- Company will provide reasonable access to facilities, systems (in non-production environments), and personnel
- Company may require auditor to use Company-provided devices and access controls
- Company may restrict access to areas containing other customers' data or Company proprietary information

#### 10.4 Audit Findings

**Remediation**:
- If audit identifies non-compliance, Company will develop remediation plan within **30 days**
- Company will implement remediation within reasonable timeframe (based on severity)
- Customer may conduct follow-up audit to verify remediation (subject to Section 10.3 conditions)

### 12. Liability and Indemnification

**Purpose**: Define liability between parties

#### 12.1 Processor Liability

**Company Liability**:
> "Company is liable for damages caused by Processing Personal Data in violation of this DPA or Data Protection Law, to the extent Company failed to comply with obligations specifically directed to Processors or Company acted outside or contrary to lawful instructions from Customer."

**Limitations**:
- Subject to limitations of liability in the Terms of Service (Section 11)
- Company is not liable for:
  - Customer's failure to comply with its obligations as Controller
  - Customer's instructions that violate Data Protection Law (if Company notified Customer)
  - Causes outside Company's reasonable control (force majeure)

#### 12.2 GDPR Liability Chain

**GDPR Article 82**:
- Under GDPR, both Controller and Processor can be held liable for damages
- If Company is held liable for damages caused by Processing where Company was not responsible, Company may claim back from Customer the part of compensation corresponding to Customer's responsibility

**Indemnification**:
- Customer will indemnify Company for claims arising from Customer's violations of Data Protection Law (e.g., unlawful instructions, failure to obtain consents)
- Company will indemnify Customer for claims arising from Company's violations of this DPA
